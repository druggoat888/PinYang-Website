Okonomiyaki

Origin

Okonomiyaki is a Japanese teppanyaki, savoury pancake dish consisting of wheat flour batter and other ingredients (mixed, or as toppings) cooked on a teppan (flat griddle).
Okonomiyaki is mainly associated with two distinct variants from Hiroshima or the Kansai region of Japan, but is widely available throughout the country, with toppings and batters varying by area.

How I enjoy...

  1. Itadakimasu!
  2. Enjoy the bottom peice of okonomiyaki first, which has lesser suace and no bonito flakes, to taste the original flavour of the okonomiyaki.
  3. Have it together with the sauce.
  4. Have it together with the sauce and the bonito flakes.
  5. Enjoy the rest of the okonomiyaki while it is still hot.

Ramen

Origin

Ramen is a Japanese noodle dish.
It consists of Chinese-style wheat noodles served in a broth; common flavors are soy sauce and miso, with typical toppings including sliced pork (chāshū), nori (dried seaweed), menma (bamboo shoots), and scallions.

How I enjoy...

  1. Itadakimasu!
  2. Savour the ramen broth first.
  3. Have a bite of the char siu, egg and other toppings.
  4. Eat the noodles with the broth.
  5. Order a plate of pan fried gyoza for a different texture.
  6. Enjoy the rest of the ramen while it is still hot.

Korean BBQ

Origin

Korean barbecue is a popular method in Korean cuisine of grilling meat, typically beef, pork or chicken. Such dishes are often prepared on gas or charcoal grills built into the dining table itself.

How I enjoy...

    • Buffet:
    1. Walk around to see what is available.
    2. Take a few plates of meat - a few of each kind, to see which one is nice.
    3. Take the following condiments if available:
      • Lettuce
      • Kimchi
      • Onion
      • Garlic
      • Enoki
      • Corn
      • Pickled radish
      • Ssamjang - soybean paste
      • Cho ganjang - soy sauce, rice vinegar, sugar, water, and other ingredients like garlic, onion, or gochugaru (chili pepper powder)
    4. Take the following side dishes if available:
      • Japchae
      • Tteokbokki
      • Pancake - kimchi/vegetable/seafood
      • Fried chicken
      • Kimchi fried rice
    5. Take drinks.
    6. Grill some of the garlic, onion and enoki. Then, grill the meat that has no marinate - such as pork belly.
    7. After a while, flip the meat over.
    8. When the meat is more cooked, use a tong to hold up the meat with my non-dominant hand, use a scissor to cut the meat into smaller pieces with my dominant hand.
    9. Let the meat cook for a while more. Flip the other condiments on the grill.
    10. Eat the side dishes while waiting.
    11. Savour a few pieces of meat without any condiments.
    12. Try a few pieces of meat with some of the sauces.
    13. Make a wrap...
      1. Take a piece of lettuce and place it flat on my non-donminant hand.
      2. Take a piece of meat, dip it into any one of the sauces and place it on the lettuce.
      3. Place any combination of the condiments on the lettuce - such as garlic, onion, kimchi and corn.
      4. Place another piece of meat on the lettuce (optional).
      5. Wrap the lettuce up and enjoy the wrap in one bite - Don't choke!.
    14. Enjoy the rest of the KBBQ and eat more.
  • À la carte:
    1. Enjoy the banchans first.
    2. Ask politely for refills.
    3. The remaining steps are the same as Step 6 - 14 above.

Thunder Tea Rice

Origin

Thunder Tea Rice, also known as Lei Cha Fan (擂茶饭) in Chinese, is a traditional Hakka dish with origins in Southern China, particularly in the Hakka community. The dish is renowned for its unique combination of rice, various vegetables, herbs, and a flavorful green tea-based broth known as "thunder tea" or "lei cha."

How I Enjoy...

Order side dishes such as niang tou fu and fried pork belly if available.

  1. Savour the the broth.
  2. Try some of the vegetables.
  3. Try the vegetables with the rice.
  4. Try the side dishes.
  5. Enjoy the rest of the TTR.

Sambal Stingray

Origin

Sambal Stingray is a popular dish commonly found in Singapore and Malaysia. It is a flavorful and spicy seafood delicacy that has its origins in the Malay and Peranakan culinary traditions. It is essentially barbecued stingray, served with sambal paste atop.

How I Enjoy...

Ask for sambal cincalok if available.

  1. Take a picture.
  2. Squeeze the lime over the sambal stingray.
  3. Savour a small piece of sambal stringray.
  4. Eat the sambal stringray with rice.
  5. Eat the sambal stringray with rice and cincalok.
  6. Eat the rest of the hawker dishes that are usually ordered with sambal stringray, such as:
    • Sambal sotong.
    • Sambal kangkong.
    • Satay.
    • Barbeque chicken wings.
  7. Enjoy the rest of the meal and make sure to eat the stringray clean.
